Equalibrium

  • Properties (measurements) do not change over obeservation time
  • e.g. $P,V,\vec{E},\vec{P}$

0th Law

  • A$\leftrightarrow$B, B$\leftrightarrow$C $\Rightarrow$ A$\leftrightarrow$B
  • As the consequence:$$\begin{align}
    A\leftrightarrow C&:F_{AC}(P_{1},V_{1};P_{3},V_{3})=0 \
    B\leftrightarrow C&:F_{BC}(P_{2},V_{2};P_{3},V_{3})=0 \
    \text{Elimate }V_{3}&:f_{AC}(P_{1},V_{1},P_{3})=f_{BC}(P_{2},V_{2},P_{3})\
    \text{And 0th Law}&:F_{AB}(P_{1},V_{1};P_{2},V_{2} ) =0
    \end{align}$$
    $$\Theta_{A}(P_{1},V_{1})=\Theta_{B}(P_{2},V_{2})$$
    We can define $\Theta$ as Temperature. And $\Theta=\Theta(P,V)$ is Equation of state
  • $T=\frac{(PV){sys}}{(PV){tri}}\times 273.16K$

1st Law

  1. Adiabatic wall: no heat exchange
    diathermic wall: can heat exchange
    isolated system
  2. State1$\stackrel{adiabatic}{\to}$State2: “Inner Energy”: $W=U(P_{f},V_{f})-U(P_{i},V_{i})$
    State1$\stackrel{diathermanous}{\to}$State2: “Heat”: $Q=(U(P_{f},V_{f})-U(P_{i},V_{i}))-W$ or $\mathrm{d}U=\mathrm{d}Q+\mathrm{d}W$
  3. Quasi-static process:
    $$\mathrm{d}W=-P\mathrm{d}V=\sum_{i}F_{i}\mathrm{d}x_{i}$$
    $F_{i}$ is the generalize force (Intensive quantity), and $\mathrm{d}x_{i}$ is the generalized displacement (Extensive quantity)
  4. Heat capacity: $C= \frac{\mathrm{d}Q}{\mathrm{d}T}\vert_{path}$
    1. Const. V: $C_{V}= \left( \frac{\mathrm{d}Q}{\mathrm{d}T} \right){V}=(\frac{\partial U}{\mathrm{\partial} T}){V}$
    2. Const. P: $C_{P}=\left( \frac{\mathrm{d}Q}{\mathrm{d}T} \right){P}=\left( \frac{\partial U}{\partial T} \right){P}+P\left( \frac{\partial V}{\partial T} \right){P}=\left( \frac{\partial H}{\partial T} \right){P}$, $H=U+PV$: Enthalpy

      e.g. Ideal Gas: Jouler’s Expension:
      $$\Delta Q=0,\Delta W=0,\Delta U=0,T_{f}=T_{i}$$
      $$\Rightarrow U(P,V)=U(T)$$
      $$C_{P}-C_{V}=P\left( \frac{\partial V}{\partial T} \right){P}=Nk{B}$$

2nd Law

  1. Kevin: No process is possible whose sole effect is complete conversion of heat to work
    Clausius: No process is possible whose sole effect is transfer of heat from colder to hotter body
  2. Carrot Engine:
    ![[Thermodynamics 2023-10-26 21.20.24.excalidraw]]
    • Cycle, reversible
    • Operates between two heat baths at temperatue $T_{H}$ and $T_{C}$
    • Carrot Thereom: Carrot is the best
    • $\eta (T_{H},T_{C})=1- \frac{T_{C}}{T_{H}}$
  3. Clausius Inequality
    • Carrot: $\frac{Q_{H}}{T_{H}}+\left( - \frac{Q_{C}}{T_{C}} \right)$ $\Rightarrow\ \sum \frac{Q_{i}}{T_{i}}=0$
    • Other cycles: $\oint \frac{\mathrm{d}Q}{T}\leq 0$ ($T$: temperature of heat bath)

Entropy

  1. Reversible: $\int _1 \frac{\mathrm{d}Q}{T}-\int _2\frac{\mathrm{d}Q}{T}=0$
    potential: entropy
    $$S(B)-S(A)=\int _A^B \frac{\mathrm{d}Q}{T} $$
    Infinitesimal:
    $$\mathrm{d}Q=T\mathrm{d}S$$
  2. Inreversible: $\int _1 \frac{\mathrm{d}Q}{T}-\int _2\frac{\mathrm{d}Q}{T}<0$
    $$\Rightarrow\int _A^B \frac{\mathrm{d}Q}{T}<S(B)-S(A) $$
    Infinitesimal:
    $$\mathrm{d}Q<T\mathrm{d}S$$
  3. Adiabatic process: $\mathrm{d}Q=0$, $\therefore \Delta S\ge0$

    • e.g.1. Jouler’s Expension:
      $$\Delta S=\int \frac{\mathrm{d}Q}{T}=\int _{V_i}^{V_j} \frac{p\mathrm{d}V}{T}=Nk_B\ln\frac{V_j}{V_i}>0$$

    • e.g.2. ![[1.Thermodynamics 2023-10-30 18.35.49.excalidraw]]
      $$T_f=1/2(T_1+T_2)$$
      Consider a const volumn process:
      $$\Delta S_1=\int _{T_1}^{T_f} \frac{C_V\mathrm{d}T}{T}=C_V\ln\frac{T_f}{T_1},\Delta S_2=C_V\ln\frac{T_f}{T_2}$$
      $$\therefore \Delta S=\Delta S_1+\Delta S_2=C_V\ln\frac{(T_1+T_2)^2}{4T_1T_2}$$

      Choose a reversible process to calculate $\Delta S$

    • $\mathrm{d}U=T\mathrm{d}S-p\mathrm{d}V$
      $U(S,V)$: natural variable S, V. $\left( \frac{\partial U}{\partial S} \right)_V=T,(\frac{\partial U}{\partial V})_S=-p$

Systems of variable particles

  • Can happen: open system; phase transition
  • $\mathrm{d}U=T\mathrm{d}S-p\mathrm{d}V+\mu \mathrm{d}N$
  • $U$: Extensivity, $U=U_1+U_2+U_3$
    1. Thermodynamic limit: $N\to\infty,V\to\infty,n=\frac{N}{V}$fixed.
    2. Short-ranged interaction
    3. Consequence of extentivity:
      $$U(\lambda S,\lambda V,\lambda N)=\lambda U$$
      $U$: 1st order homogeneous function.
      $\lambda$: scale factor.
      $$\Rightarrow U=TS-PV+\mu N$$
      Differential and $\mathrm{d}U=T\mathrm{d}S-p\mathrm{d}V+\mu \mathrm{d}N$
      __Gibbs-Duhem relation: __$S\mathrm{d}T-V\mathrm{d}p+N\mathrm{d}\mu=0$

Thermodynamic potentials

  1. $U=U(S,V,N)$, we are at liberty to choose other variables.
  2. Enthalpy: $$H=U+pV\Rightarrow\mathrm{d}H=T\mathrm{d}S+V\mathrm{d}p+\mu \mathrm{d}N$$
    $$\therefore H=H(S,p,N)$$
  3. Helmholtz Free Energy: $$F=U-TS\Rightarrow\mathrm{d}F=-S\mathrm{d}T-p\mathrm{d}V+\mu \mathrm{d}N$$
    $$\therefore F=F(T,V,N)$$
  4. Gibbs Free Energy: $$G=F+pV\Rightarrow\mathrm{d}G=-S\mathrm{d}T+V\mathrm{d}p+\mu \mathrm{d}N$$
    $$\therefore G=G(T,p,N)$$
  5. Grand potential: $$\Psi=F-\mu N=U-TS-\mu N\Rightarrow\mathrm{d}\Psi=-S\mathrm{d}T-p\mathrm{d}V-N \mathrm{d}\mu=-p\mathrm{d}V-V\mathrm{d}p$$
    (Gibbs-Duhem relation)

Towards Equal

  1. $U,V$ fixed, $\Delta S\geq 0$
  2. $T,V$ fixed, $\Delta F=\Delta U-T\Delta S=Q-T\Delta S\leq 0$
  3. $T,P$ fixed, $\Delta G=\Delta U-T\Delta S+p\Delta V=Q-T\Delta S\leq 0$

Maxwell’s Relation

$$\left( \frac{\partial U}{\partial S} \right){V}=T,\ \left( \frac{\partial U}{\partial V} \right){S}=-p,\ \therefore \frac{\partial ^{2}U}{\partial S \partial V}=\left( \frac{\partial T}{\partial V}\right){S}=-\left( \frac{\partial P}{\partial S} \right){V}$$
Relate quantities to experimentally measurable quantities.

  • e.g. Heat Capacity
    $$C_{V}=\left( \frac{\partial U}{\partial T} \right){V}=T\left( \frac{\partial S}{\partial T} \right){V},C_{p}=\left( \frac{\partial H}{\partial T} \right){p}=T\left( \frac{\partial S}{\partial T} \right){p}$$
    $$\text{And }\left( \frac{\partial S}{\partial T} \right){p}=\left( \frac{\partial S}{\partial T} \right){V}+\left( \frac{\partial S}{\partial V} \right){T}\left( \frac{\partial V}{\partial T} \right){p}$$
    $$\therefore C_{p}-C_{V}=T\left( \frac{\partial S}{\partial V} \right){T}\left( \frac{\partial V}{\partial T} \right){p}=T\left( \frac{\partial p}{\partial T} \right){V}\left( \frac{\partial V}{\partial T} \right){p}$$
  • Response Function
    1. Extensivity: $\alpha=\frac{1}{V}\left( \frac{\partial V}{\partial T} \right)_{p}$
    2. Conpressibility: $\kappa_{T}=- \frac{1}{V}\left( \frac{\partial V}{\partial p} \right){T}$
      $$\therefore C      {p}-C_{V}=\frac{TV\alpha^{2}}{\kappa_{T}}$$

Phase Equalibrium

  • Partition into uniform subsystem.
    $$S=\sum_{a}S^{a}(U_{a},V_{a},N_{a})$$
    Maximize $S=\sum_{a}S^{a}$, subject to constraint:
    $$\begin{equation}
    \left{
    \begin{array}{lr}

\sum_{a}U_{a}=U \
\sum_{a}V_{a}=V \
\sum_{a}N_{a}=N
\end{array}
\right.
\end{equation}
$$

  • Lagrange Multiphier:
    $$f=\sum_{a}S^{a}(U_{a},V_{a},N_{a})-\alpha\left( \sum_{a}U_{a}-U \right)-\beta\left( \sum_{a}V_{a}-V \right)-\gamma\left( \sum_{a}N_{a}-N \right)$$
    $$\therefore \frac{\partial f}{\partial U_{a}}=0,\frac{\partial f}{\partial V_{a}}=0,\frac{\partial f}{\partial N_{a}}=0,\ \textit{And }\ \mathrm{d}S=\frac{1}{T}\mathrm{d}U+\frac{p}{T}\mathrm{d}V-\frac{\mu}{T}\mathrm{d}N$$
    $$\Rightarrow \frac{1}{T_{a}}=\alpha, \frac{p_{a}}{T_{a}}=\beta, -\frac{\mu_{a}}{T_{a}}=\gamma$$
    $$\therefore T_{1}=T_{2}=\dots=T, \ p_{1}=p_{2}=\dots=p, \ \mu_{1}=\mu_{2}=\dots=\mu$$
  • Stability condition: $S$ is maximiazed.
    $$S=\sum_{a}S^{a}(U_{a},V_{a},N_{a})\ \therefore \sum\left( \frac{\partial ^{2}S}{\partial x_{i}\partial x_{j}} \right)\delta x_{i}\delta x_{j}\leq 0,\ x_{i}\in {U_{a},V_{a},N_{a}} \textbf{ (Hessian matrix)}$$
    $$\frac{\partial ^{2}S}{\partial x_{i}\partial x_{j}}\delta x_{i}\delta x_{j}=\frac{\partial }{\partial x_{j}}\left( \frac{\partial S}{\partial x_{i}} \right)\delta x_{i}\delta x_{j}=\delta J_{i}\delta x_{i}\ \left( J_{i}=\frac{\partial S}{\partial x_{i}}\text{: conjugate variable} \right)$$
    $$\text{And: }\mathrm{d}S=\frac{1}{T}\mathrm{d}U+\frac{p}{T}\mathrm{d}V-\frac{\mu}{T}\mathrm{d}N$$
    $$\therefore \sum_{i}\delta J_{i}\delta x_{i}\leq 0 \Leftrightarrow \delta\left( \frac{1}{T} \right)\delta U+\delta\left( \frac{p}{T} \right)\delta V+\delta\left( -\frac{\mu}{T} \right)\delta N\leq0$$
    $$\therefore \delta T\delta S-\delta p\delta V+\delta \mu \delta N\geq 0$$
    e.g. $\delta N=0\Rightarrow \delta T\delta S-\delta p\delta V\geq 0$
    Choose $(T,V)$ as independed variables: $S=S(V,T),P=P(V,T)$
    $$\text{So: } \delta S=\left( \frac{\partial S}{\partial T} \right){V}\delta T+\left( \frac{\partial S}{\partial V} \right){T}\delta V,\ \delta p=\left( \frac{\partial p}{\partial T} \right){V}\delta T+\left( \frac{\partial p}{\partial V} \right){T}\delta V$$
    $$\Rightarrow\left( \frac{\partial S}{\partial T} \right){V}(\delta T)^{2}+\left[ \left( \frac{\partial S}{\partial V} \right){T}-\left( \frac{\partial P}{\partial T} \right){V} \right]\delta T\delta V-\left( \frac{\partial p}{\partial V} \right){V}(\delta V)^{2}\geq 0$$
    $$\text{Maxwell Relation: }\left( \frac{\partial S}{\partial V} \right){T}=\left( \frac{\partial P}{\partial T} \right){V},\left( \frac{\partial S}{\partial V} \right){T}=\frac{C{V}}{T},-\left( \frac{\partial p}{\partial V} \right){T}=\frac{1}{V\kappa{T}}$$
    $$\therefore C_{V}\geq 0,\ \kappa_{T}\geq 0$$
